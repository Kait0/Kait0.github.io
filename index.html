Research Project: "Novel View Synthesis on KITTI-360"
Oct 2020 - Jan 2021

Novel View synthesis is a technique that tries to render a scene from new angles given a set of images.
Publications in that field usually evaluate their methods on proprietary data making it hard to compare different works or to measure the progress of the field.
In this research project I propose to use a new self-driving dataset to benchmark these methods.
To that end I implemented a naive baseline and reproduced the state-of-the-art method on the new dataset.
Additionally, I discuss the possibility to extend the work of the field to synthesize semantic segmentation labels.

Bachelor Thesis: "Measuring Google QUIC Connection Establishment Times"
Apr 2018 - Aug 2018

I investigated the then new Google QUIC protocol which was deployed by Google on a production level scale on the internet to establish connections between clients and servers.
Since this was done by a private company it was unclear how this new protocol compared to existing methods.
I developed tools to measure the connection establishment time of this protocol and competing methods (TCP + TLS 1.2 / 1.3).
These tools were then used to conduct an empirical study that showed that Google QUIC establishes connections faster than TCP/TLS.

Seminar: "Examining Hoeffding Trees with SHAP"
Apr 2020 - Jul 2020

Hoeffding Trees are tree models designed to learn from large datastreams.
In theory they are supposed to converge towards a Decision Tree.
With the new Tree SHAP algorithm for explaining tree models I show that this is not always the case in practice.
